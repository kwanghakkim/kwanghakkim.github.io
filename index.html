I am a fourth year Ph.D. student in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at UC San Diego (UCSD), where I am advised by Prof. Miroslav KrstiÄ‡ and Prof. Mamadou Diagne. My research focuses on nonlinear and safety-critical control of nonholonomic systems, with an emphasis on control Lyapunov and barrier function (CLF/CBF) methods for systems with state constraints, actuation constraints and uncertainty. I work on problems including nonholonomic vehicle parking, robust safety filters for moving obstacles, and high-relative-degree safety constraints, with applications to autonomous ground vehicles and aircraft-carrier deck operations. Recently, my work has developed analytic feedback designs for nonholonomic robots that are safe, inverse-optimal, adaptive, and guarantee convergence in fixed or prescribed time.

I hold an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UC San Diego and a B.S. in Aerospace Engineering from The University of Texas at Austin. Prior to my doctoral studies, I conducted undergraduate research in autonomous systems under Prof. Ufuk Topcu and in experimental mechanics at Seoul National University.
